## Mechanisms of Climate Action Delay: From Denial to Sophisticated Obstruction

### Executive Summary
Research into climate action obstruction reveals a significant evolution from traditional climate denial (questioning the existence or cause of climate change) to more sophisticated "discourses of delay" and "climate obstruction." This research identifies a multi-dimensional landscape of tactics used to hinder climate mitigation and adaptation, categorized into three primary targets: the climate phenomenon itself, proposed climate actions/solutions, and the credibility of climate actors/research [1]. Key mechanisms include **greenwashing** (e.g., "nature-rinsing" and "bridge fuel" myths), **delayism** (e.g., "doomism," "whataboutism," and "policy imperfection" arguments), **science skepticism** (attacking research validity and consensus), and the exploitation of **culture wars** and **conspiracism** (e.g., "climate scam" narratives and "Great Reset" conspiracies) to polarize public discourse [1].

---

### Findings

#### 1. Greenwashing and False Solutions (Target: Climate Action)
Obstructionists frequently promote "false solutions" that anchor societies in the status quo or perpetuate fossil fuel dependence [1].
- **"Bridge Fuel" and Fossil Fuel Expansion**: Natural gas is frequently framed as a "bridge fuel" to a renewable future, a narrative used to justify continued investment in fossil fuel infrastructure [2, 6]. This is often paired with "greenwashing" or "nature-rinsing" tactics—using imagery of nature or vague sustainability claims to distract from massive fossil fuel expansion [1].
- **Corporate Net-Zero Claims**: Many corporations make "net-zero" pledges that lack substance, relying on creative accounting or unverified carbon offsets rather than absolute emissions reductions [8, 10]. Some companies, such as Shell, have been accused of promoting "net-zero" content while internally acknowledging it is not a core business plan [1].
- **Technological Fundamentalism**: Proponents of "false solutions" often rely on "technological fundamentalism," arguing that future innovations like Carbon Capture and Storage (CCS) or geoengineering will solve the crisis, thereby making immediate political or systemic intervention unnecessary [1].

#### 2. Delayism and Economic Panic (Target: Climate Action)
Delayism shifts the focus from whether climate change is happening to why action should be postponed [1].
- **Economic Arguments and "Harmfulness"**: A prominent delay tactic is the argument that climate action is "too expensive" or will cause "significant economic losses" and restrict individual liberties [1, 3]. This is often framed as a "harmfulness" argument, weighing the perceived costs of transition against its benefits [1].
- **Responsibility Shifting ("Whataboutism")**: Obstructionists often use "whataboutism" to deflect responsibility, pointing to the emissions of other nations (e.g., "China and India must act first") to justify domestic inaction [1].
- **"Doomism" and "Inefficiency"**: "Doomism" suggests that climate action is futile because it is "too late," while other arguments claim that mitigation efforts are "inefficient" or will produce only "marginal results," thereby legitimizing inaction [1].
- **Policy Imperfection**: Actors exploit perceived flaws in climate policy—such as a lack of public consultation or social acceptability—to "disorient and discourage" ambitious action [1].

#### 3. Science Skepticism and Credibility Attacks (Target: Phenomenon and Source)
Obstruction has moved from denying the phenomenon to undermining the sources of knowledge [1].
- **Phenomenon Obstruction**: This includes "trend skepticism" (denying warming is occurring) and "severity skepticism" (challenging the negative impacts of climate change) [1].
- **Attacking Research Credibility (Source Credibility Obstruction)**: Tactics include attacking the methods, data, and reliability of climate models ("junk science") and questioning the motivations or funding of scientists [1].
- **Conspiracism**: A significant rise in "conspiratorial" framing has linked climate action to the "Great Reset," "New World Order," or "global elites" attempting to exert control or destroy capitalism [1]. The term "#ClimateScam" has emerged as a high-traction narrative on social media to delegitimize climate science [1].

#### 4. Regulatory Capture and Identity Politics (Target: Action and Source)
- **Culture War Integration**: Climate obstruction is increasingly being absorbed into broader "culture wars," where climate action is framed as "elitist," "anti-woke," or a threat to "traditional ways of life" [1].
- **Identity and Partisanship**: Arguments often appeal to national, cultural, or partisan identities to frame climate policies as threats to individual freedom, the free market, or even "Western civilization" [1].
- **Regulatory Capture**: There is evidence of industry-funded efforts to influence the judiciary and policy through "healthy skepticism" training and the promotion of industry-aligned narratives [7].
