## Rolling Back Gender Equity and LGBTQ+ Targeting: Research Report

### Executive Summary
As of May 2026, research indicates a multi-faceted movement to rollback gender equity and restrict LGBTQ+ rights through both legislative action and digital rhetoric. This rollback manifests in several key dimensions: the legal dismantling of protections for transgender individuals through Supreme Court rulings and state-level legislation; the use of "Red Pill" and "Manosphere" ideologies to normalize misogyny and hyper-masculinity; the systematic deconstruction of reproductive healthcare access via proposed federal policies like "Project 2025"; and the use of medical misinformation to delegitimize gender-affirming care. A significant trend is the use of "religious liberty" and "free speech" as legal frameworks to defend practices such as conversion therapy and to bypass established medical standards of care.

### Findings

#### TRANS PANIC and LGBTQ+ Targeting
- **Legislative Surge**: In 2025, the ACLU documented 616 anti-LGBTQ+ bills in the U.S., a trend that escalated into early 2026 with coordinated state-level surges often utilizing model bills from organizations like the Alliance Defending Freedom [6, 7].
- **Medical Misinformation**: There is a significant rise in politically motivated misinformation targeting gender-affirming care [10]. Common false narratives include claims that gender-affirming surgeries are being performed on children in schools—a claim debunked by fact-checkers—and that such care is "experimental" or causes high rates of "regret" [10].
- **Dehumanization and Erasure**: Recent scholarship suggests a shift from the criminalization of gender-diverse people to their "erasure and dehumanization" in all forms of life, particularly through policy frameworks like Project 2025 [3].

#### MISOGYNY and "Red Pill" Ideology
- **Digital Manosphere**: The "Red Pill" ideology has moved from internet subcultures to influencing broader societal views on gender roles and power dynamics [1]. It is characterized by a belief that biology determines dominance, with men being naturally superior to women [2].
- **Algorithmic Amplification**: Social media platforms use recommendation systems that push young men toward increasingly extreme content, often repackaging misogyny as "self-improvement," "humor," or "relatable" content [9].
- **Cultural Masculinity**: Digital subcultures, including the "Incel" community, contribute to a landscape of "reactionary masculinity" that critiques feminism and promotes hierarchical gender roles [5, 8].

#### REPRODUCTIVE RIGHTS and "Project 2025"
- **Project 2025 Agenda**: This far-right policy framework seeks to dismantle sexual and reproductive health and rights (SRHR) through several aggressive measures [Guttmacher]:
    - **Medication Abortion**: Proposes revoking FDA approval for mifepristone and reinstating restrictions that would effectively end telehealth provision for abortion [Guttmacher].
    - **Institutional Rebranding**: Suggests renaming the Department of Health and Human Services to the "Department of Life" and creating an anti-abortion task force [Guttmacher].
    - **Language Erasure**: Recommends deleting terms such as "gender," "gender equality," "reproductive health," "reproductive rights," "abortion," "sexual orientation," and "gender identity" from all federal legislation, rules, and agency websites [Guttmacher].
- **Rhetorical Strategies**: Anti-abortion advocacy often uses "charged, medically inaccurate rhetoric" to portray abortion as unsafe, aiming to break down support for rights and increase the criminalization of providers [Guttmacher].

#### CONVERSION THERAPY and Legal Challenges
- **Supreme Court Ruling (Chiles v. Salazar)**: On March 31, 2026, the U.S. Supreme Court ruled in *Chiles v. Salazar* that Colorado's ban on conversion therapy for minors was "likely unconstitutional" because it regulated speech based on viewpoint [4, 5]. The Court held that the ban, as applied to "talk therapy," infringed upon the First Amendment rights of licensed professionals [4, 8].
- **Religious Liberty Defense**: Legal challenges to conversion therapy bans frequently argue that such bans violate free speech or religious freedom protections [2, 5].
- **Impact on Healthcare**: Dissenting opinions in *Chiles* argue that this ruling may create an era of "unprofessional and unsafe medical care" by making speech-only therapies effectively unregulatable by state licensing or malpractice standards [4, 31].

#### PAY GAP DENIAL
- **Narrative of "Choice"**: Academic research highlights how the "rhetoric of choice" is used to minimize the impact of structural inequality. By attributing the gender wage gap to individual decisions (e.g., choosing lower-paid roles, part-time work, or remaining childless), organizations can obscure systemic failures and structural power imbalances [1, 2].
