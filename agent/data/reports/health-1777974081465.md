## Erosion of Trust in Public Health and the Rise of Unproven Treatments

### Executive Summary
The erosion of trust in public health institutions is a multifaceted phenomenon driven by the proliferation of misinformation, the politicization of medical guidance, and the economic exploitation of health anxieties. Key drivers include the misrepresentation of surveillance data (specifically VAERS), the spread of unfounded "gene therapy" claims regarding mRNA technology, and the rise of a lucrative "wellness" industry that promotes unproven "detox" and supplement-based treatments. This distrust is further exacerbated by political shifts, such as the discontinuation of federal mRNA research funding, and the framing of public health mandates as infringements on personal liberty.

### Findings

#### Vaccine Misinformation and Data Misrepresentation
- **VAERS Misuse**: The Vaccine Adverse Event Reporting System (VAERS) is frequently misrepresented by vaccine skeptics. While VAERS is an open surveillance system for reporting unconfirmed and unverified medical events, it is often used to fuel vaccine hesitancy by presenting unverified reports as causal evidence [1, 5]. Scholars have proposed rebranding the system to a "Vaccine Safety Sentinel" to reduce public susceptibility to deceptive interpretations [1].
- **mRNA "Gene Therapy" Myths**: Widespread misinformation has targeted mRNA technology, with claims that these vaccines function as "experimental gene therapy" or can alter human DNA [2, 3]. Such misconceptions are often driven by the rapid development and emergency use authorization of mRNA-based COVID-19 vaccines, creating a "novelty penalty" that erodes confidence [2].
- **Discrepancy in Risk Perception**: Social media discourse often focuses on severe, rare side effects (e.g., myocarditis, death) that are discussed disproportionately compared to the actual frequency of these events in official VAERS data, which typically shows common, mild reactions like fatigue and headache [2].

#### Quackery and the "Wellness" Economy
- **The "Detox" Trend**: A multi-billion dollar "detox" industry has emerged, promoted by influencers and wellness brands. These products, such as "detox teas" or cleanses, often claim to "flush toxins" from the body without scientific evidence [8].
- **Supplement Marketing**: The dietary supplement market, exceeding $60 billion annually, faces significant regulatory challenges. Many products are marketed with unproven health claims, and some contain ingredients that have been banned by the FDA [4].
- **Monetization of Health Anxiety**: The wellness industry often preys on genuine human desires for control and longevity, turning "optimization" into a lucrative business model that can sometimes lead to "toxic" community environments where unverified medical advice is echoed without critique [8, 9].

#### Medical Skepticism and Political Polarization
- **"Big Pharma" Conspiracies**: Public distrust is frequently fueled by theories alleging that pharmaceutical companies prioritize profit over patient safety, manipulating clinical trial results or withholding negative data [5, 9].
- **Politicization of Public Health**: Public health measures, such as mask mandates and vaccine requirements, are increasingly framed as "encroachments on liberty" or tools of political control [3, 6]. This polarization can lead to significant health risks, including the obstruction of beneficial legislation and decreased adherence to evidence-based guidelines [9].
- **Impact of Policy Shifts**: In 2025, the discontinuation of federal mRNA research funding—driven by leadership changes at the NIH and HHS—has been cited as a consequence of manufactured distrust and political skepticism regarding mRNA technology [3].
